Research

My work lies at the interface of mathematical physics and algebra, with particular interests in topological quantum field theory, tensor categories, and related structures arising in topological phases and lattice models.

Broadly speaking, I am interested in the mathematical structures underlying topological phases of matter and quantum field theory. My recent work focuses on quantum double models, weak Hopf symmetry, tube algebras, and related categorical constructions.

Introduction to 2D Topological Quantum Field Theory

Fall 2026 (planned)

This course provides a mathematically rigorous introduction to two-dimensional topological quantum field theory (2D TQFT), with an emphasis on its algebraic and categorical structures. We introduce cobordisms and their organization into symmetric monoidal categories, leading to the functorial definition of TQFT in the sense of M. Atiyah. The central theme is the classification of 2D TQFTs via Frobenius algebras, following Kock’s book.

This course is intended for advanced undergraduate students and graduate students in mathematics. It serves as an entry point to modern interactions between topology, algebra, and mathematical physics.

  • Cobordism categories and symmetric monoidal functors
  • Duality and trace in monoidal categories
  • Frobenius algebras and diagrammatic calculus
  • The correspondence between 2D TQFTs and commutative Frobenius algebras

Prerequisites: Linear algebra, abstract algebra, and basic topology. Some familiarity with category theory is helpful but not required.

References:

  • Joachim Kock, Frobenius Algebras and 2D Topological Quantum Field Theory, Cambridge, 2003.
  • Michael Atiyah, Topological Quantum Field Theories, Publications Mathématiques de l'IHÉS, 68 (1989), 175–186.
